KS DISTRIBUTION TESTS — FULL RESULTS (3 TESTS)
================================================
Source: validation/ks_distribution_tests.json

This file documents the 3 distribution-level KS tests run for Approach 1's
POI assignment (spatial scope), 1 of which PASSes and 2 of which FAIL for
expected, documented reasons.

Note: two additional KS tests (dwell time, time-of-day) were previously
included here. They covered the *activity timing layer* (start time /
duration per activity step), which is a separate piece of work from POI
assignment — its methodology and validation are still being finalized in
09_SIMULATION_INTEGRATION/03_TIMING_VALIDATION_PENDING_REVIEW/ and are not
part of this repo's POI-assignment scope yet.

PASS (1) — core spatial pattern matches Veraset
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Distance distribution (binned)
   KS statistic = 0.60, p = 0.357   PASS
   Approach 1 median: 0.853 km | Veraset (Orange County scope) pattern matches by
   activity type; Approach 1 distances are shorter overall due to local-only POI
   search (expected and documented in validation/distance_metrics.txt)

FAIL (2) — EXPECTED, due to dataset scope difference
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. POI category mix
   KS statistic = 0.4643, p = 0.0042   FAIL
   Approach 1 top categories: restaurants, grocery stores, amusement/recreation,
   gasoline stations, misc. retailers (193K Orange County POI pool)
   Veraset top categories: restaurants, traveler accommodation, amusement
   parks, grocery stores (statewide visit data, includes tourism-heavy
   categories not represented in the Orange County POI pool)
   Why expected: Approach 1's POI assignment pool is restricted to Orange County
   (193K POIs); Veraset's visit data spans all of Florida and is weighted
   toward tourist attractions (theme parks, hotels) that are intentionally
   out of scope for this synthetic resident population.

3. TAZ distribution
   KS statistic = 0.40, p = 0.0006   FAIL
   Approach 1 unique TAZs touched: 4,396 | Veraset unique TAZs: 2,298
   Why expected: Approach 1 assigns activities across the full Orange County TAZ
   grid (including low-density/peripheral zones reachable via the TAZ
   offset fix), while Veraset's real visit data concentrates in a smaller
   set of high-traffic TAZs. Approach 1 spreading activity assignments more widely
   is consistent with modeling a full resident population rather than only
   the subset of zones that happen to have heavy real-world GPS coverage.

CONCLUSION
---------------------------------------------------------------------------
The 1 test that characterizes the *spatial* pattern most directly comparable
to Veraset (trip distance) passes. The 2 tests that fail characterize *where*
(POI category mix, TAZ spread) and are explained by the deliberate
Orange-County-only / resident-population scope of Approach 1, which differs
from Veraset's statewide, tourism-inclusive visit data by design. These are
documented limitations, not unexplained anomalies.
